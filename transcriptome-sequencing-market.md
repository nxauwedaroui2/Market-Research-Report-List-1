<p><p>The transcriptome sequencing market encompasses two primary types: coding RNA and noncoding RNA. Coding RNA refers to messenger RNA (mRNA) that translates into proteins and plays a crucial role in cellular functions. Noncoding RNA, on the other hand, includes various RNA molecules that do not translate into proteins but are vital for regulatory processes, such as microRNAs and long noncoding RNAs. Each segment serves distinct applications in research, diagnostics, and therapeutics, advancing our understanding of gene expression and regulation.</p></p>
