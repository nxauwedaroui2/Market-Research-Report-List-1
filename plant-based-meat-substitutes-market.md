<p><p>The plant-based meat substitutes market features several key types. Tofu-based products, made from soybeans, offer a versatile protein source. Tempeh-based substitutes, also soy-derived, are fermented for added nutrition and flavor. TVP (textured vegetable protein) is made from soy flour and is known for its meat-like texture. Seitan, made from wheat gluten, provides a chewy, meat-like consistency. Quorn, derived from mycoprotein, is a unique fungi-based option. Other substitutes may include ingredients like legumes, grains, or novel protein sources.</p></p>
