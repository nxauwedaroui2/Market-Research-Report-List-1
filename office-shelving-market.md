<p><p>In the office shelving market, there are two primary types: open and closed shelving. Open shelving typically features exposed storage spaces, allowing easy access and visibility of items, promoting collaboration and quick retrieval. It is often used for books, files, and decorative items. In contrast, closed shelving provides a more organized and formal appearance, with cabinets or doors that conceal contents, enhancing privacy and protection for sensitive materials. Both types serve distinct purposes based on organizational needs and design preferences.</p></p>
