<p><p>The paint thinner market comprises various types, each serving distinct purposes. Mineral spirits are commonly used for clean-up and thinning oil-based paints. Acetone is known for rapid evaporation, making it ideal for nail polish remover and cleaning. Turpentine, derived from pine trees, serves both as a solvent and a paint thinner. Naphtha is favored for its effectiveness in degreasing and thinning. Toluene and Methyl Ethyl Ketone (MEK) are used in industrial applications for their strong solvent properties. Dimethylformamide (DMF) and 2-Butoxyethanol offer alternatives for specialty applications, while others include various formulations tailored for specific tasks.</p></p>
