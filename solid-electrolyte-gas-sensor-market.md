<p><p>The Solid Electrolyte Gas Sensor market is classified into several types based on the materials used. Sulfate-based sensors utilize sulfates for their ionic conductivity. NASICON-based sensors leverage sodium superionic conductors for high-performance gas detection. β-Al2O3-based sensors employ a beta-alumina structure, known for its ion-conducting properties. LaF3-based sensors utilize lanthanum fluoride, providing unique detection capabilities. The "Others" category encompasses alternative solid electrolyte materials used in niche applications, expanding the versatility and functionality of gas sensing technologies.</p></p>
