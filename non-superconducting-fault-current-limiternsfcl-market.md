<p><p>Non-Superconducting Fault Current Limiters (NSFCL) are devices designed to protect electrical systems from fault currents by limiting their magnitude. The market features two primary types: Saturable Core NSFCL, which utilize magnetic materials that saturate to limit current flow during faults, ensuring system safety and stability; and Solid State NSFCL, which employ advanced semiconductor technology to quickly respond to fault conditions, providing increased reliability and faster reaction times. Both types enhance power system resilience while minimizing operational disruption.</p></p>
